Who we are.

Get Bridgend Online is a project where different organisations work together aiming to get the whole of Bridgend digitally included. The partner organisations involved are Communities 2.0, TimecentresUK, Valleys2Coast, The Local Service Board and Bridgend Council. This project fits in the Digital Inclusion Framework, as set up by the Welsh government in 2010.

Digital technologies are ever-increasing and changing fast. The internet is a part of everyday life and is used for many different reasons, whether it be economical (e.g. searching for work, online shopping), socially (e.g. using Skype) or for public services. That means digital skills have now become as essential as reading and writing. However, it is estimated that about 34% of the Welsh adult population does not know how to use the internet or do not have access to it. People who are not able to use the internet, miss out on many things, making them socially and economically disadvantaged. That is why the Digital Framework Wales was set up by the Government in 2010. Get Bridgend Online is extremely proud to be part of this!

The team consists of six paid staff and a large number of Digital Champions , working in different venues across Bridgend borough. We all love technology and we love helping people, and our job is a combination of the two. So come and meet us at one of those venues or contact us via this website or social media.

What we do.

Everyday, we are out and about teaching people how to get online and how to make the most of the internet. We cater for all abilities and ages. Our sessions are always free, informal and can be taught on a one-to-one basis or in a group. You can even bring your own equipment (tablets, computers, phones etc.) or you are more than welcome use ours.

An important part of our work is helping people use the Government Gateway website, which gives access to Universal Credits (applying for benefits, searching for work etc.)

We are also happy to be involved in helping people in sheltered housing schemes, libraries and community centres. Anyone can come to these sessions and ask us their questions related to digital skills. In short; we are not telling you what to do, you tell us what you want to learn and we take it from there.

Topics we regularly cover are:

  • Basic computer skills
  • Online shopping
  • Facebook
  • Skype
  • Gaming
  • Photoshop
  • Computer security

A new element of the Get Bridgend Online initiative is the training of front-line staff. We give the framework on how to teach digital skills to people they work with on an everyday basis. This includes Jobcentre staff and sheltered housing scheme staff. This enables us to reach even more people. Please contact us for further information.

We could not do this without our Digital Champions. They give some of their time every week to help others get online. They come from all walks of life, and besides a good knowledge of all the basic digital skills, they each have their own area of expertise.

Becoming a Digital Champion couldn't be easier. Training is normally no more than 2 days and will give you all the ‘knowhow’ needed to start helping people get online in your community.

In addition to the training, we also offer a number of digital rewards and incentives. Please contact us if you would like more information about becoming a Digital Champion.

Get Bridgend Online Launch Party

Posted 16th December 2013.

On Friday the 6th of December Get Bridgend Online had its launch party.

The party kicked off with awards and workshops given by Carwyn Jones AM. The event was attended by members of the press and government, curious members of the public, beneficiaries and Digital Champions.

Awards were given to both Digital Champions and beneficiaries by Carwyn Jones along with presentations of identification badges for the latest additions to the army of Digital Champions.

Junior Digital Champion 2013 was awarded to Matthew Barnard for his outstanding contribution to the community in the areas of Digital Inclusion. Senior Digital Champion 2013 awarded to Michael Kenny for his outstanding contribution to the community in the areas of Digital Inclusion. The Digital Learner of the Year 2013 was awarded to Lynne Williams for using the skills she acquired to improve her life, both personally and professionally.

Silver Surfer Award was awarded to Ray Mackey for now knowing more than the ‘Average Joe’ with regards to mobile phones. Beneficiaries Champion 2013 was awarded to Craig Williams as voted for by the beneficiaries on the Digital Inclusion Project 2012 -2013. Commitment Award 2013 was awarded to Karl Clarke for always giving support and dedication, both in and out of the classroom.

The workshops led by the Digital Champions demonstrated the following technologies and the skills involved with them.

  • Green Screen Photography
  • Using PC’s
  • Playing Xbox Kinect
  • Using Tablets
